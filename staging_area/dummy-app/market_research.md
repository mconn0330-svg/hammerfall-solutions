# Dummy App — Market Research
**Author:** Scout
**Date:** 2026-03-27

## Market Overview
The task management space is heavily saturated, but increasingly fragmented into two extremes:
1. **Consumer/Generalist:** Todoist, TickTick (too much visual fluff, mouse-dependent)
2. **Enterprise/Team-Focused:** Linear, Jira, Asana (bloated with assignees, sprints, comments)
3. **Hyper-Niche Developer:** Taskwarrior, CLI tools (steep learning curve, no web/mobile visibility)

## The Opportunity (The "Solo Dev" Niche)
Solo engineers want the raw speed of a CLI tool (Taskwarrior) paired with the omnipresence and visual accessibility of a Web App. They hate taking their hands off the keyboard. They do not care about "mentions," "collaborators," or "workspaces."

## Competitor Analysis

| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses (for our niche) |
|---|---|---|
| **Linear** | Incredible keyboard navigation, fast, beautiful UI | Designed 100% for teams. Too much mental overhead for solo devs. |
| **Taskwarrior** | Terminal native, infinitely extensible, lightning fast | CLI-only. Hard to view on mobile. Syntax takes memorization. |
| **Things 3** | Beautiful execution, polished | Mac ecosystem only. Mouse-heavy. No CLI. |
| **Superlist** | Great design | VCs, collaboration, bloated for solo use. |

## Core Positioning
**"Just you, your tasks, and your terminal."**
A web-based task manager that forces a keyboard-first paradigm. Every action is a keystroke. The aesthetic is terminal-native (`monospace`, high contrast). An official companion CLI interacts with the exact same database.
